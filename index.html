Diana Travel tour

Rwanda has become among the most loved and visited safari destination in Africa. The country being so much known as the smallest and smart country in East Africa, has attracted so many luxury hotels, lodges and exclusive camps in the safari parks and now it is one of the newly luxury destinations in Africa. That ever-growing travel wish list might be putting some pressure on your pocket but there are plenty of places to tour in Rwanda where you will get more bang for your buck.

Day Trip Rwanda Gorilla Trekking

Volcanoes National Park is situated about two hours from Kigali city, Rwanda’s capital. The drive to the park is scenic – viewing the beautiful mountains and green forests, which gave Rwanda its name as the “land of a thousand hills.” Rwanda Gorilla Trekking can take minutes or hours, depending on where the gorillas decide to rest from the previous day. In Rwanda Gorilla Trekking experience, you will have a magical chance to see them feeding and crushing through the underbrush, babies running around and full-grown gorillas inching toward observers loudly declaring their importance.

Two Day Nyungwe National Park

This 2 Days Rwanda Wildlife Safari rewards the best wildlife viewing Rwanda chimpanzee trekking experience in Nyungwe National Park. This national park offers sights of more 730 African Birds species and The forest is rich in botanical diversity hosting over 200 species of trees and flowering plants and about 300 bird species.
The forest is also home to 13 species of primates including Olive baboons, L’Hoest’s monkeys, Ruwenzori colobus, Vervet monkeys, silver monkey, Grey-cheeked manga-bey, red-tailed monkey etc. You will most likely sight some of these forest inhabitants on your Rwanda chimpanzee trekking.

Three Day Akagera National Park

his 3 Days Akagera National Park Wildlife Safari gives you a great experience to explore this park. You will enjoy Game drive, boat trip and night game drive. Akagera national park is known for harboring the big 5 with the recent introduction of the lions. This park the home to several mammal species including Lions, Elephants, Giraffes, Hippos, Kobs, Zebras, Bushbucks, Waterbucks and most recently Black Rhinos have been introduced, making it to host the big five….
